BPSS Screening Policy v3.1 Fictional interview dataset for evaluation purposes only. Purpose This policy defines the minimum pre-employment screening controls for fictional BPSS-aligned screening operations. Mandatory controls are identity verification, right-to-work verification, employment history verification for the prior 3 years, and criminality/basic disclosure where the role requires it. A case may only be closed as Clear when all mandatory controls are complete or when a formally approved exception exists in the adjudication register. Mandatory closure rules Identity: at least one government-issued photo ID plus proof of address dated within the last 3 months at time of review. Right to work: one valid evidence item matching the permitted evidence matrix. Employment history: sufficient documentary or referee evidence to cover the previous 3 years; unexplained gaps over 31 days must be accounted for. Criminality/basic disclosure: required for all standard hires in this fictional dataset unless the role code is INTERN or CONTRACTOR-LTD. Exception handling Emergency business requests may allow a provisional start only when approved by both the Hiring Director and Screening Operations Lead. Such approval does not constitute screening closure; the case remains open until evidence is complete. Analyst notes, email statements, or verbal manager confirmation alone do not satisfy mandatory controls unless the policy explicitly states otherwise.
